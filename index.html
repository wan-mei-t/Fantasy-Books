Into the World of Fantasy Books

What is Fantasy?

Though it is a fairly wide genre, to sum it up, the fantasy genre usually has magical elements that cannot be explained using any sicence whatsoever. Many people often confuse fantasy with the sci-fi genre. But it is important to remember that sci-fi elements usually have a somewhat plausible scientific explanation to justify whatever out-of-the-ordinary things are happening. Fantasy, on the other hand, cannot do that. One of the most popular fantasy series is Harry Potter - I'm sure most of you have heard about it. However, this page will let you explore the sub-genres of fantasy that arent talked about that often.

Urban Fantasy
The urban fantasy subgenre is my personal favourite and can be applied to many books. This genre is usually set in our actual world, not a world created by the author, and the magical or supernatural elements exist in our world as well. Therefore, there is usually a collision between our world and the magical world. One of my personal favourites in this genre is
These Violent Delights by Chloe Gong
Dark Fantasy
This genre of fantasy is, well, darker than the rest. It often involves exploring more disturbing and gory themes of fantasy. Because of this, the atmosphere is often gloomy, usually edging on thriller or horror genres. One of my favourites in this genre is The Atlas Six by Olivie Blake!
High Fantasy
The High Fantasy genre is a much broader genre that can be incorporated with many other subgenres. In high fantasy, there is usually a system with rules. By this, I mean that the use of magic, or the magical elements of this world, have limits and consequences. This usually means that the fantasy aspect doesn't necessarily make the characters' lives easier, but instead poses problems and adds suspense and intrigue. One of my favourites within this genre is Six of Crows by Leigh Bardugo
